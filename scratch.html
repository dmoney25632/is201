Rockets & Space Launches

A passion for rockets, space exploration, and the companies pushing humanity beyond Earth.

Why Rockets?

Rockets are the most powerful machines humans have ever built. From the Saturn V that sent astronauts to the Moon to SpaceX's reusable Falcon 9, the history of rocketry is a story of ambition, engineering, and relentless iteration. We are living through a second space race — this time driven by private companies as much as governments.

Rocket launches have become routine — but never boring.

Types of Rockets

  1. Orbital Rockets
    • Designed to reach orbit around Earth or beyond
    • Examples: Falcon 9, Atlas V, Ariane 5, Long March
    • Must achieve ~17,500 mph (orbital velocity)
  2. Suborbital Rockets
    • Reach space but do not complete a full orbit
    • Examples: Blue Origin New Shepard, Virgin Galactic
    • Used for tourism, research, and testing
  3. Heavy Lift Vehicles
    • Carry very large payloads to orbit
    • Examples: SpaceX Falcon Heavy, NASA SLS, Soviet N1
    • Critical for deep space missions and large satellites
  4. Reusable Rockets
    • Can land and be relaunched — dramatically cuts costs
    • SpaceX pioneered this with the Falcon 9 first stage
    • Starship is designed to be fully reusable
